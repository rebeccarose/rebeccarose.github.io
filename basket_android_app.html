Problems

Obesity rates are rising with fewer at-home meals

Home-cooked meals are declining in the US. Households now spend more than 40% of their total food budget on foods prepared outside of the home, up from 25% in 1970. Studies have shown that the foods people select when they eat out generally have more calories, fat, and saturated fat than at-home meals and snacks. (Source: United States Department of Agriculture Economic Research Service, Eating Out Increases Daily Calorie Intake)

Meal planning requires time and energy

Planning and creating a shopping list for at-home meals requires effort. When shoppers don’t plan their meals, they can overestimate how many perishables they will consume, and the food is eventually thrown out.

Food waste is destroying the environment

In the US, organic waste is the second highest component of landfills, which are the largest source of methane emissions. 30-40% of the food supply is wasted, equaling more than 20 pounds of food per person per month. (Source: United Nations Environment Programme, Regional Office of North America, Food Waste: The Facts)

Opportunity

Millennials could drive healthier food consumption

Millennials are now the largest generation represented in the US and their spontaneous approach to food consumption will continue to have a big impact on our culture and environment. Targeting this audience would yield significant results.
